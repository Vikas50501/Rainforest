Things to See Around Munnar

Munnar is a must-see tourist spot in God's own country, located in the Idukki district of Kerala. Sitting deep among tea gardens, misty viewpoints, waterfalls and wildlife, it is one of the most loved hill stations in South India — and every attraction below is a short, scenic drive from the resort.

The pride of Munnar

The Neelakurinji Bloom

Munnar is home to the highest concentration of Neelakurinji plants in the country, spread over 3,000 hectares of rolling hills. Each shrub flowers only once in its lifetime and then dies; it takes another twelve years for the seeds to sprout and grow 30–60 cm high for the next glorious bloom. The Neelakurinji belongs to the genus Strobilanthes — of about 450 species worldwide, 146 are found in India and around 43 in Kerala. This rare bloom earned the Western Ghats the fourth spot in Lonely Planet's 2018 Best in Asia.
