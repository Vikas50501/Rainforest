The Kashmir of the South

A Misty Village in the Western Ghats

Kanthalloor is a village known as the Southern Kashmir — nestled in the Western Ghats of India, situated in Kerala around 50 km from Munnar. This is the only place in Kerala where the ambient temperature does not go beyond 19°C.

The salubrious climate, picturesque landscapes and a diversity of tropical crops have transformed this misty hill village into a premier destination — presenting visitors with a rare opportunity to encounter nature on the intimate scale of the High Ranges.

The farm stay

A Two-Bedroom Country Farm House

At Rainforest Kanthalloor Orchard we offer a unique two-bedroom country farm house experience within an exclusive organic farm. To preserve the special character crafted for genuine enthusiasts, this facility is available in a limited and controlled manner during certain seasons.

Here we have recreated the farm-house concept — a replica of how our older generations lived in harmony with nature. This two-acre fertile land is abundant with fruit-bearing trees rare to be found elsewhere in India, along with exotic vegetables and herbs.

Things to do

Experiences & Activities

From orchard tours to sandalwood-forest walks and tribal-settlement visits — the hills are yours to explore.

Orchard & Garden Tour

Walk through our organic orchard, spice garden and vegetable plots — learn about each plant from our farmer guide.

Mountain Trekking

Trek through misty mountain trails and explore Manavan Shola National Park by jeep — pure wilderness awaits.

Wildlife & Nature

Visit Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, sandalwood forests, the Muniyaras and Pattisseri Dam — all within close range.

Photography & Bird Watching

The mist-shrouded valleys and diverse bird life make Kanthalloor a paradise for photographers and birdwatchers.

Local Market Shopping

Browse fresh spices, honey, tea, handicrafts and prized sandalwood oil from the local Kanthalloor markets.

Tribal Village Visit

Visit the Kulachuvayal tribal settlements (Muthuvan people) and the traditional jaggery production unit nearby.
